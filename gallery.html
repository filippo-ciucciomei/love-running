When I joined my most recent company as Head of Customer Service, I inherited a team without structure, clear roles, or measurable objectives. The environment was reactive and demotivating, with inconsistent service and low morale. As the only senior leader with operational and customer expertise, I was responsible for diagnosing the issues and leading a full restructure.

I began by meeting each team member individually to understand their challenges, capabilities, and aspirations. This helped build trust and ensured that the changes were shaped by frontline insight, not assumptions. I mapped workflows end-to-end, identified duplicated effort and gaps in accountability, and restructured the function into specialist sub-teams with defined responsibilities and success measures.

To embed the new structure, I introduced regular one-to-ones, team briefings, and clear performance expectations. I also created progression paths and coaching plans to increase engagement and give staff a sense of direction. Throughout the process, I communicated openly about the reasons for the changes and encouraged feedback to ensure the team felt included and supported.

The impact was immediate: collaboration improved, response times fell, and customer satisfaction rose consistently over the following months. Staff reported feeling more confident and valued, and the team operated with greater ownership and clarity.

This reinforced my belief that strong leadership means setting a clear vision, empowering people, and creating the conditions for them to succeed — principles I would bring to leading operational teams within HMCTS.
